Key Policies

  • Food Security & Social Welfare: Strengthen the Public Distribution System (PDS) to ensure food security for all. Expand and universalize social welfare schemes for the poor, elderly, and marginalized communities.
  • Employment & Wages: Expand MGNREGA to 200 workdays per year with increased wages. Guarantee employment for urban poor through an Urban Employment Guarantee Act. Ensure minimum wages and job security for all workers, including those in the informal sector.
  • Public Sector & Economy: Stop privatization of Public Sector Units and strategic national assets. Promote public investment in infrastructure, manufacturing, and agriculture. Support small and medium enterprises and cooperative movements.
  • Education: Ensure free, quality, and universal education from primary to higher levels. Increase public spending on education to 6% of GDP. Oppose commercialization and saffronization of education.
  • Healthcare: Ensure free, universal, and quality healthcare for all citizens. Strengthen public health infrastructure, expand insurance coverage, and regulate private healthcare providers.
  • Land & Agrarian Reforms: Implement land reforms, protect farmers' rights, ensure fair prices for crops, and expand irrigation and rural credit. Oppose corporate takeover of agriculture and promote sustainable farming.
  • Women & Social Justice: Enforce laws for women's safety, representation, and equal pay. Promote reservation for women in legislatures and jobs. Fight for the rights of Dalits, Adivasis, minorities, and LGBTQ+ communities.
  • Environment: Promote renewable energy, protect forests and water bodies, and oppose environmentally destructive projects. Ensure climate justice and support communities affected by climate change.
  • Governance & Democracy: Ban corporate funding of political parties. Strengthen anti-corruption laws, protect whistleblowers, and ensure transparency in governance. Safeguard federalism and decentralize power to local bodies.
  • Foreign Policy: Pursue an independent foreign policy based on peace, non-alignment, and solidarity with progressive movements worldwide. Oppose imperialism and defend national sovereignty.
  • Digital Rights & Internet Access: Ensure affordable, universal access to the internet and digital technologies. Protect data privacy, net neutrality, and digital rights. Oppose internet shutdowns and surveillance without due process.
  • Urban Development: Promote affordable housing, public transport, and sustainable urban planning. Upgrade slums, ensure clean water and sanitation, and protect the rights of urban poor and migrant workers.
  • Youth & Students: Expand scholarships, skill development, and job opportunities for youth. Ensure student representation in decision-making and protect campus democracy. Oppose commercialization of education and student debt.
  • Science & Technology: Increase investment in scientific research, innovation, and technology for public good. Promote open access to knowledge and support indigenous scientific talent.
  • Culture & Heritage: Protect and promote India's diverse cultural heritage, languages, and arts. Oppose cultural homogenization and censorship. Support artists, writers, and cultural workers.

These policies reflect CPI(M)'s commitment to building a just, equitable, and inclusive India where the needs of the people come first.
